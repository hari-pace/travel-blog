Mauritius

posted by Hari, July 2023

Background to the trip

In April 2023 my partner and I got married, and after a few years exploring different places around Europe, we decided to go a little further afield for our honeymoon. With the beautiful weather, crystal-clear waters and pristine beaches, we decided that Mauritius would be the perfect destination for 8 days of pure relaxation.

The long(ish) journey

First time using Emirates Airlines

Flight from Munich to Dubai

Flight from Dubai to Mauritius

Considering it was my first time leaving Europe, and therefore my first long-distance flight, I really could not complain about the journey. As there are no direct flights, we had to travel from Munich to Dubai and then from Dubai to Mauritius, with a three hour stop-over in-between. The total time in the air was around 12.5 hours, but with the comfortable atmosphere, tasty food and world-class on-board entertainment system (I could even watch live Premier League football on the plane!), that 12.5 hours really 'flew by'. With the host of offerings at the shiny new Dubai airport, we also had no problem keeping entertained during the 3 hour stop-over.

The dream hotel

Le Morne mountain behind the hotel

Pool which we didn't use, prefer the ocean!

Dinner with a view

While there is no shortage of quality hotels on the island, we were blown away by the image (see above) of the Le Morne mountain overlooking the hotel, which sits directly on the waterfront. After seeing this, we knew this is where we wanted to spend our honeymoon. It's safe to say that we were not disappointed!

The all-inclusive food options were amazing, offering both local and international dishes. There were multiple restaurants on-site to ensure that guests never got bored.

Activities

Stand-up paddling

First time waterskiing!

Tennis in front of Le Morne mountain

The hotel also had a large offering of leisure activities to use free of charge. In the 8 days we were there, we managed to go stand-up paddling, waterskiing and on a glass-bottom boat, as well as playing tennis, table tennis and using the spa. The 'honeymoon package' that we booked included a complimentary massage and yoga session, so we made sure to take advantage of that.

Things to do

  • Swim in the ocean
  • Try the local food (a blend of African, Chinese, European (mainly French) and Indian cuisines)
  • Check out the national park
  • Try some water sports
  • Visit a temple

Good to know

  • The local currency is the Mauritian Rupee
  • Pack an umbrella - it's rare but when it rains, it really rains!
  • Hire a car in advance if you require one
  • Most people on the island will speak both English and French
  • The high season is between October - April and the low season is between May - September, although it is warm all year round.
